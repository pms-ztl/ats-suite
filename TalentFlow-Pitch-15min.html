The five lines that win the room

Whoever says it, say it slowly, then stop talking. Let it land.

  1. "Not a number. The sentence."Danish · the proof behind every score (Scene 4)
  2. "Two rupees to read a resume a person gives six seconds."Danish · the cost (Scene 3)
  3. "The AI advises. You decide. And it is always saved."Preethi · a human stays in charge (Scene 4)
  4. "Fairness as a live dial, not a once-a-year report."Preethi · the fairness check (Scene 4)
  5. "A data leak between companies simply cannot happen."Sanjay · the database wall (Scene 6)
What is real, so you stay safe under questions. Everything you click is real: the form builder and Publish, the four ways candidates come in, the bulk resume upload, the auto-filled Parsed résumé, the pass / review / decline score with the exact proof line, the Review Queue, the live fairness (Adverse impact) tab, the Settings, Team page with roles and Reports-to, the Billing and Plan usage and plan change, the Compliance Audit log, and the Data retention controls. Two things are framed as "available," not demoed as magic: the AI picking interview times by itself (that needs your calendar connected) and deeper background checks of a candidate's claims (off by default). And the cost per resume is the month's real bill divided by resumes read, not a per-resume price tag in the app. Keep to that and the pitch stays jaw-dropping and survives every follow-up.
